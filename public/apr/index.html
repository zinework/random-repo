02.5 · How It Was Taken
كيف استُولي عليها

Not a Conflict.
A Colonial Project.

The media calls it a conflict. A conflict implies two equal sides. This is a one-sided settler colonial project, built in stages by empires who never asked.

1899

The First Warning

Yusuf Diya, the mayor of Jerusalem, wrote to the founder of the Zionist movement objecting to its plans for Palestine. Palestinians saw this coming long before the world paid attention. (Khalidi, 2017)

1916

Sykes-Picot Agreement

Britain and France split the Middle East into zones without consulting a single Palestinian. Most of Palestine went to direct British rule. (Khalidi, 2017)

1917

The Balfour Declaration

Britain promised a homeland for the Zionist movement on land it didn't own. Zionists backed by the British military were able to slowly transform the 94% Arab majority Palestine into a Jewish state. (Khalidi, 2017)

" We do not propose even to go through the form of consulting the wishes of the present inhabitants. Arthur Balfour
1947

The UN Partition Plan

Jewish land ownership was only 11.8%, yet the UN partition gave a majority share to expand the Jewish state. (Salem, 2002)

1948

The Nakba

Jaffa, 120,000 people, emptied in a week. 700,000 Palestinians expelled. ~500 villages destroyed. Many left holding their house keys. (Al Jazeera, 2017)

1967

Military Occupation Begins

Israel takes the West Bank, Gaza, and East Jerusalem in six days. Settlement construction starts immediately and never stops. Over 700,000 Israeli settlers now live on Palestinian land in the West Bank.

1993

Oslo: Peace Deal That Wasn't

The Oslo Accords promised statehood. Instead Palestinians got a governing body with no real sovereignty while Israel kept control of borders, water, and military. Settlement construction actually sped up after Oslo.

2007

Gaza Blockade

A comprehensive land, air, and sea blockade locks 2.3 million people inside Gaza. The UN calls it collective punishment. Electricity for a few hours a day. The Rafah crossing, the only exit, frequently shut.

2023

The War on Gaza

4 of 400 aid sites still operating. 50% of hospitals, 81% of roads, 92% of water infrastructure, destroyed. Israeli planned projects picture Gaza as a vacation spot.

2026

Today

Canada recognized Palestine in 2025, yet still sends arms to Israel. The same ideologies destroying Gaza work to destroy Palestinian identity in North America.

Somewhere over the Atlantic…
A family carries a house key to a home in Jaffa they have never been allowed to return to.

The Nakba scattered Palestinians across the world. Many found new homes in Canada. But Canada did not welcome their history. The dispossession that started in 1948 did not end at the border.

🏍️
Lived Experience

"I put a Palestinian flag on my motorcycle. The next day, it was gone. I assumed someone at the university parking lot had taken it. It was my mother. She said: 'I want to see you come home safe.'"

She was implying that if someone on the road had seen a Palestine flag, they would be more inclined to cause an accident. Palestinian identity is not just erased in institutions. It is treated as a safety risk by the people who love you most.

🧣
Oakville, April 2024

A guidance counsellor at Iroquois Ridge High School told a student that his keffiyeh "reminded her of a terrorist."

The Halton District School Board investigated and called it "harmful and discriminatory anti-Palestinian racist language." The student was simply wearing a scarf. The same week, the Ontario legislature banned keffiyehs from Queen's Park. (CBC News, April 2024)

05.5 · Here, Too
هنا أيضاً

The Silence Canada Asks For

Even though many Palestinians in Canada don't face bombed hospitals, they still face anti-Palestinian racism as their history is being silenced by institutions, media and society. The Nakba was not just something in the past for them; many live with the stories from their parents and grandparents who migrated to Canada.

The Nakba Denial

Canada Does Not Recognize the Nakba

Canada still does not recognise the Nakba as a significant world event. Some members of the NDP party have recognised it and made several statements on it (CJPME, 2023). The Canadian government's denial of the Nakba is itself a form of anti-Palestinian racism (CJPME, 2022). It denies them and silences their Palestinian heritage and culture. For a long period of time, Canada was unable to recognise Palestine as its own state until 2025, yet Canada still sends arms to Israel, allowing a blockade on food supplies. (Wildeman, 2025)

The Palestine Exception

Solidarity Becomes Extremism

Canadians who speak out about how Palestine is being wronged, whether at school or university, are often labelled as antisemitic and can be fired or kicked out of schools. Non-Palestinians are stripped away from telling Palestinian stories too, such as CBC journalists who were in severe trouble when they decided to support the Palestinian cause. (Zine, RE339)

McGill 2024

Tear Gas on Campus

During a protest at McGill in 2024, police forces were shown using brutal force and tear gas against attendees when one person broke a window. Later on, police were kicking down people's tents, damaging belongings and bashing protestors. One of the main reasons students are at the forefront of protesting is due to the connection of these institutions to Zionism as a whole. (Lau et al., 2024)

The Toxic Other

The Toxification of the Palestinian Other

Palestinians and their allies are constructed as a dangerous presence, what Ayyash calls the "toxification" of the Palestinian other (Ayyash, 2022). Social media amplifies this by justifying Israeli settler violence as self-defence while soldiers mock the destruction of Palestinian homes. Even wearing a kufiyeh or saying the word "Nakba" gets reframed as a security risk. Palestinians in Canada face an umbrella of hatred: anti-Palestinian racism, anti-Arab racism, and Islamophobia. All three target their right to wear their kuffiyeh, have the Nakba acknowledged, or protest without consequences. (Zine, 2024)
